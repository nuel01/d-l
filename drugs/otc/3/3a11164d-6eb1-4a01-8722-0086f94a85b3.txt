Folic acid alone is improper therapy in the treatment of pernicious anemia and other megaloblastic anemias where Vitamin B12 is deficient .
Folic acid especially in doses above 1 mg daily may obscure pernicious anemia in that hematologic remission may occur while neurological manifestations remain progressive .
Concomitant use of ginger in patients with bleeding disorders , or who are on anti - coagulant or anti - platelet therapy , may increase the risk of bleeding .
